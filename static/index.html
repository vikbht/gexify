Understanding the Chart

  • Positive GEX (Calls): Market Makers are long gamma. This acts as a dampener on volatility. Price tends to mean-revert or stay pinned near these strike levels, acting as strong support/resistance.
  • Negative GEX (Puts): Market Makers are short gamma. This amplifies volatility. They are forced to sell when the market drops and buy when it rises, potentially accelerating directional breakouts.
  • Net DEX (Delta Exposure): Shows the aggregate directional bias (bullish vs bearish) of option positioning. Positive DEX implies overall call dominance; Negative DEX signifies put dominance.
